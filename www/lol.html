My Work

MOND, Galaxy Dynamics, TDG's etc...

Elliptical galaxies have been suspected for a long time to have formed with a top-heavy IMF (Matteucci 1994, A&A) and to have formed within less than a Gyr (Thomas et al. 2005; Recchi et al. 2009, A&A). The early and rapid formation time scale has been difficult to understand and simulations of galaxy formation have not been taking into account a possibly evolving galaxy- wide IMF. In particular, none of the existing hydrodynamical simulations ensure consistency ofthe galaxy-wide IMF with the IMF found in star-forming molecular cloud cores. This project aims to do exactly this for the first time. \n

The novel aspect of my research will be to incorporate into the self-consistent simulations of the formation and evolution of elliptical galaxies an IMF which depends on the local density of gas and on its metallicity. This will provide a non-linear feedback since, in the here to be adopted formulation, the number of massive ionizing stars increases strongly relative to the number of low mass stars at low metallicity and high density, thus providing a stronger feedback regulation of star formation. This is likely to have a strong effect on the final galaxy properties, and I want to investigate this effect in comparison to a non-varying (i.e. standard) IMF.
