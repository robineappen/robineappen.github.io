About Me

My Work

My first major study investigated the monolithic collapse of post-Big-Bang gas clouds in Milgromian gravity. This showed that early-type galaxies (ETGs) can form rapidly with star formation timescales (SFTs) aligning with observations, challenging hierarchical merging. (*Eappen et al. 2022, MNRAS*)

Next, I studied the formation of compact massive relic galaxies â€” ancient, unchanged systems like NGC 1277. MOND reproduces these relics naturally. (*Eappen 2024, MNRAS*)

Recently, I explored the morphological evolution of ETGs under MOND, showing that mergers of relics yield realistic ellipticity, rotation, and scaling relations, including the Fundamental Plane and Kormendy relation. (*Eappen & Kroupa 2025, Galaxies*)
